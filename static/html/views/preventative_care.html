Ways to Prevent Oral Cancer
Evidence-based prevention strategies
Stop Tobacco Use

Tobacco (smoking or chewing) is the #1 risk factor for oral cancer. Quitting reduces risk by up to 50% within 5 years.

Critical
Limit Alcohol Intake

Heavy drinking increases oral cancer risk 6x. Combined with tobacco, risk multiplies up to 30x. Moderation is key.

Important
Eat a Healthy Diet

Fruits and vegetables rich in antioxidants (vitamins A, C, E) protect oral cells. Avoid excessive sugar and processed foods.

Recommended
Visit Your Dentist Regularly

Regular check-ups (every 6 months) enable early detection. Dentists can spot pre-cancerous lesions before symptoms appear.

Essential
Brush 2x Daily
After breakfast & before bed, for at least 2 minutes each.
Floss Daily
Removes 40% of plaque your brush can't reach between teeth.
Stay Hydrated
Water washes away bacteria & keeps saliva levels healthy.
